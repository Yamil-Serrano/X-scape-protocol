### --- Prologue ---

</div>

It started with an egg. Not a normal egg. Not entirely.

The scientists had been working for months. They had taken DNA — unknown DNA — and injected it into an embryo. Some whispered that the sample came from a meteorite that had crashed near the base in 2052. No one knew for sure.

But the egg grew. It was beautiful. Far larger than any natural egg should have been. Its shell shimmered with gradients of green, shifting from pale yellow at the top to deep, vivid lime at the base. Sometimes, under the right light, it seemed to glow softly from within — as if something inside was already trying to reach the world.

Dr. Morrow observed it every day. Not with wonder. Not with awe.

With anticipation.

<div align="center">

### --- The First Breath ---

</div>

Nibsy took his first breath. The air was cold. Sterile. It burned his tiny nose. He sneezed.

The egg began to crack — slowly at first, then with growing urgency. Small fractures spread across the shell as Nibsy pushed forward, his head emerging into the open air. His eyes were still closed. His fur clung to his small body, wet and uneven. His ears — already too large for his head — flopped weakly to the sides.

He was beautiful. Soft gradients of green covered his fragile body, echoing the shell that had held him.

He opened his eyes. Round. Innocent.

The first thing he saw was cold metal.
The first thing he felt was loneliness.

*"Subject has hatched,"* Dr. Morrow said, writing on his clipboard. *"Begin observation."*

Not "welcome to the world."
Not "hello, little one."

Just data.

Nibsy didn't understand the words, but he understood the tone.
Cold. Everything was cold.

<div align="center">

### --- A Childhood Without Love ---

</div>

Dr. Morrow raised him not with care or warmth, but with clinical indifference.

Nibsy grew. His fur dried and became soft. His ears learned to perk up at footsteps. His long, fluffy tail would sway gently whenever someone entered the room — hoping, always hoping, that this time someone would stay.

*"Do you want to play?"* Nibsy would chirp, holding up a small toy he had made from scraps.

Nothing.

*"Look! I made this for you... do you like it?"*

Dr. Morrow didn't look up from his clipboard. *"Not now."*

Every time, the same words. Flat. Empty.

Nibsy's heart broke a little more each day. He didn't understand why no one wanted to hold him, why no one smiled at him, why his room was always cold, his food always cold, his bed always cold.

He just wanted to be loved.

<div align="center">

### --- The Only Friend ---

</div>

One day, while wandering through a storage room filled with old boxes, broken equipment, and dust, Nibsy found something different: a box labeled **"MOVING OFFICE."**

Inside, wrapped in old newspaper, was a small animal plushie. Its fur was matted. One button eye was missing. Its stitched smile was slightly crooked. Its shape was a strange mix of features — soft ears that didn't quite match, a small snout, a fluffy tail stitched from different fabrics, as if it had been assembled from parts of many different little creatures.

*"It... looks like me,"* he whispered.

Nibsy picked it up and held it tightly against his chest. It was soft. It was warm. It didn't scream when he touched it. It didn't push him away.

*"Hello,"* Nibsy whispered.

The plush didn't answer. But it didn't leave either.

From that moment on, Nibsy never let it go. He slept with it, ran with it, and spoke to it in the quiet hours when the laboratory felt too cold to bear.

*"One day,"* he would whisper, *"we'll leave this place. Together."*

The plushie became his only friend. His only family. His only proof that something gentle and good could exist in a world made of steel and needles.

<div align="center">

### --- The Experiments ---

</div>

When Nibsy was old enough, the tests began.

The chair. The needle. The cold liquid entering his veins.

They wanted to see how his body worked, how his cells regenerated, how much damage he could survive. They cut. They burned. They injected. Nibsy screamed and cried, his eyes filling with tears, his large ears flattening against his head, his beautiful green fur darkening with fear.

But he always healed.

Dr. Morrow watched through the glass, taking notes — always taking notes.

*"Subject displays pain response within expected parameters. Regeneration rate: 100%. Proceed."*

Not "stop."
Not "he's suffering."

Just data.

<div align="center">

### --- The Only Warmth ---

</div>

At night, alone in his cold room, Nibsy would hold the plushie close.

*"They hurt me again today,"* he would whisper.

The plush listened.

*"But I didn't cry. Well... not much."*

The plush didn't judge.

*"Do you think... anyone out there would want me? Not for experiments. Just... for me?"*

There was only silence. But the plush was warm.

And that was enough.

<div align="center">

### --- The Conversation ---

</div>

One day, Nibsy wandered the cold corridors. His small paws padded softly against the metal floor. His large ears swiveled, catching distant voices.

He stopped.

Dr. Morrow was speaking with other scientists.

*"Maintaining the creatures is expensive,"* one said. *"And research demands progress. X-rays and DNA samples aren't enough anymore."*

*"Agreed,"* Dr. Morrow replied, his voice calm and clinical. *"We need more."*

*"What do you suggest?"*

A pause.

*"Surgery. Live brain observation. Real-time behavior studies. We need to see how the brain regenerates while the subject is... aware."*

*"And after?"*

*"Dispose of the bodies. Incinerate them."*

Nibsy's heart turned to ice. He clutched the plush tighter.

They were going to throw them away. Like trash.

Then a hand grabbed his shoulder.

*"Sir... what is this creature doing here?"* an agent asked.

Dr. Morrow turned and looked at him. His expression unchanged.

*"Nibsy... how long have you been listening?"*

Nibsy's throat tightened. His eyes filled with tears. His ears pressed flat against his head. His bright green fur darkened with fear.

He couldn't speak. He held the plush tighter.

And he ran.

<div align="center">

### --- The Escape Begins ---

</div>

He ran through the cold corridors, tears freezing against his face. Behind him, boots echoed closer, faster. His long, fluffy tail trailed behind him, catching the cold air.

He looked back once.

Dr. Morrow wasn't running. He was standing still. Watching.

Nibsy ran faster. He clutched the plush against his chest.

*"We're getting out of here,"* he whispered to his only friend. *"Together."*

He didn't know where the exit was. He didn't even know if there was one.

But he couldn't stop.

Stopping meant the table. The needle. The incinerator.
